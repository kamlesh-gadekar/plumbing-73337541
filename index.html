How do I know if I have a hidden water leak in my home?
Common signs of a hidden water leak include an unexplained spike in your water bill, the sound of running water when all fixtures are off, damp or discolored spots on walls or ceilings, and mold or mildew growth. You can also monitor your water meter — if it continues to move after all water sources are turned off, you likely have a leak. QuickFix Plumbing uses advanced leak detection technology, including acoustic sensors and thermal imaging, to pinpoint leaks without unnecessary damage to your property. Early detection is key to preventing costly structural damage and mold issues. If you suspect a leak, call us at 512-555-0199 right away.
What should I do if my drain is clogged and won't clear with a plunger?
If a standard plunger isn't resolving your clog, it's likely that the blockage is deeper in the drain line or is caused by a buildup of grease, hair, soap scum, or even tree roots. Avoid using chemical drain cleaners, as they can corrode your pipes over time and rarely solve serious clogs. Our professional plumbers use hydro-jetting and motorized drain snakes to thoroughly clear blockages and restore proper flow. We can also perform a video inspection to identify any underlying issues contributing to recurring clogs. Give QuickFix Plumbing a call and we'll have your drains flowing freely in no time.
How long does a water heater typically last, and when should I consider replacing mine?
Traditional tank water heaters generally last 8 to 12 years, while tankless models can last 15 to 20 years with proper maintenance. Signs that your water heater may need replacement include rusty or discolored hot water, rumbling or popping noises, inconsistent hot water temperatures, and visible corrosion or leaking around the unit. If your water heater is approaching the end of its expected lifespan or requiring frequent repairs, replacement is often more cost-effective than continued maintenance. QuickFix Plumbing offers a wide selection of energy-efficient water heater options, including tankless units, to suit your household's needs and budget. How can I prevent plumbing problems in my Austin home year-round?
Regular plumbing maintenance is the best way to avoid unexpected breakdowns and costly repairs. We recommend scheduling an annual plumbing inspection to check for leaks, inspect water heater performance, test water pressure, and assess drain health. In Austin, it's especially important to protect your pipes during winter cold snaps by insulating exposed pipes and knowing how to shut off your main water supply. Avoid flushing anything other than toilet paper, and use drain screens to catch hair and debris.
