Signs Your Water Heater Needs Attention

Many water heater problems give early warning signs before a complete failure occurs. Watch for these indicators that it's time to call 512-555-0199:

  • No hot water or inconsistent temperatures — If your water is lukewarm, runs cold quickly, or fluctuates unexpectedly, the heating element, thermostat, or gas burner may be failing.
  • Discolored or rusty water — Reddish or brown hot water typically signals internal tank corrosion or a failing anode rod — a serious issue that requires prompt attention.
  • Popping, rumbling, or banging sounds — Sediment buildup from Austin's moderately hard water causes these sounds and significantly reduces efficiency and lifespan.
  • Water pooling around the unit — Any visible leaking from the tank, pressure relief valve, or connections is an urgent warning sign.
  • Rising energy bills — An aging or sediment-clogged water heater works harder and costs more to operate. Efficiency losses can be dramatic.
  • Unit age over 10 years — The average tank water heater lasts 8–12 years. If yours is approaching or past that range, proactive replacement is often more cost-effective than repairs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a water heater replacement take in Austin?
A standard tank water heater replacement typically takes 2–3 hours from start to finish, including disconnecting the old unit, installing the new one, making all connections, and testing the system. Tankless installations may take 3–5 hours depending on whether gas line or electrical upgrades are needed. In most cases, you'll have hot water the same day you call.
Should I repair or replace my water heater?
As a general rule, if your water heater is less than 8 years old and the repair cost is less than 50% of a new unit's cost, repair is usually the better choice. If the unit is over 10 years old, has a leaking tank, or requires a repair approaching the cost of replacement, we typically recommend replacing it. Our technicians will give you an honest assessment with both options and their costs so you can make an informed decision.
What size water heater do I need for my Austin home?
The right size depends on how many people live in your home and your peak usage patterns. General guidelines are: 1–2 people = 30–40 gallons; 3–4 people = 40–50 gallons; 5+ people = 50–80 gallons. For tankless systems, sizing is based on flow rate (gallons per minute) rather than storage capacity. Our technicians will calculate the correct size for your specific household during the estimate.
Are tankless water heaters worth the higher upfront cost in Austin?
For most Austin homeowners, yes. Tankless systems last 20+ years (compared to 10–12 for tank units), deliver unlimited hot water, and can reduce water heating energy costs by up to 34% for the average household according to the U.S. Department of Energy. Austin's relatively mild winters also mean gas tankless units perform efficiently year-round. The higher upfront cost is typically recovered within 5–8 years through energy savings.
Does QuickFix Plumbing offer any warranty on water heater installations?
Yes. All water heaters we install come with the manufacturer's warranty (typically 6–12 years on the tank and 1–5 years on parts depending on the model). In addition, QuickFix Plumbing provides a 1-year labor warranty on all installations. If anything related to our workmanship causes an issue within that period, we return and correct it at no charge.
Why does my water heater make banging or popping sounds?
Banging or popping sounds from a water heater are almost always caused by sediment — primarily calcium and magnesium deposits from Austin's moderately hard water — settling at the bottom of the tank. As the burner heats water trapped beneath the sediment layer, it bubbles and pops through. This reduces efficiency, increases energy costs, and accelerates tank wear. A professional flush and descaling service can resolve the issue; however, if sediment buildup is severe, it may indicate the tank is nearing the end of its serviceable life.
