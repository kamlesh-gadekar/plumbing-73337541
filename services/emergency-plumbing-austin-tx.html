Common Plumbing Emergencies We Handle

Our emergency plumbers are equipped and trained to handle the full spectrum of urgent plumbing situations, including:

  • Burst or frozen pipes — Austin winters can deliver unexpected freezes. When a pipe bursts inside a wall or under a slab, water damage accumulates rapidly. We locate the break, shut off the supply, and make a lasting repair.
  • Sewage backups and overflows — Raw sewage in your home is both a health hazard and a structural threat. We use camera inspection and hydro-jetting to clear the blockage and sanitize the area.
  • Major water leaks and flooding — Whether from a failed supply line, a broken appliance connection, or a collapsed drain, we stop the flow and assess the damage.
  • Gas line leaks — If you smell rotten eggs, evacuate immediately and call us. Our certified technicians handle gas line repairs safely and efficiently.
  • No hot water — A failed water heater can disrupt your entire household. We repair or replace units on the same visit whenever possible.
  • Overflowing toilets — A single blocked toilet can quickly become a multi-room disaster. We clear the clog and inspect the drain line to prevent recurrence.

What To Do While You Wait for Our Emergency Plumber

Taking the right steps before our plumber arrives can significantly reduce water damage:

1. Locate and shut off your main water valve. In most Austin homes, this is near the street or in the garage. 2. Turn off the electricity in any rooms where water is pooling to prevent electrocution. 3. Move valuables, electronics, and furniture away from the affected area. 4. Do not use any drains or toilets if you suspect a sewer backup. 5. Take photos of the damage for your homeowner's insurance claim.

Frequently Asked Questions

How quickly can QuickFix Plumbing respond to a plumbing emergency in Austin?
In most Austin zip codes, we arrive within 60 minutes of your call. Our dispatch team is live 24/7, so when you call 512-555-0199, a real person answers and sends the nearest available plumber immediately. Response times may vary slightly during major storms or city-wide freeze events.
Does QuickFix Plumbing charge extra for late-night or weekend emergency calls?
We do have after-hours rates for calls outside standard business hours, but we are completely transparent about them. Your plumber will provide a full written estimate before starting any work, so you'll know the exact cost upfront with no surprises on your final bill.
Where is my main water shut-off valve located?
In most Austin homes, the main shut-off valve is located near the front of the house by the street (in a covered box at ground level), in the garage, or near the water heater. If you can't find it, our dispatcher can walk you through locating it over the phone while your plumber is en route.
What qualifies as a plumbing emergency?
Any situation involving active water flow you cannot stop, sewage backing up into your home, suspected gas leaks, loss of all water service, or flooding should be treated as an emergency. When in doubt, call us — we'd rather assess a situation and give you peace of mind than have you wait and risk serious damage.
Can QuickFix Plumbing help with insurance claims after water damage?
Yes. Our technicians document damage thoroughly and can provide detailed written reports, photos, and itemized repair invoices that are commonly required by homeowner's insurance companies. We work with many Austin-area insurance adjusters and can help streamline the claims process for you.
Do you handle gas line emergencies as well as water plumbing?
Yes. QuickFix Plumbing has certified technicians qualified to work on natural gas and propane lines. If you smell gas, leave the building immediately, avoid using any electrical switches, and call 911 first if you believe there is immediate danger. Then call us at 512-555-0199 and we will dispatch a gas-certified plumber right away.
