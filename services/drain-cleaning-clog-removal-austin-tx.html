Professional Drain Cleaning in Austin, TX

A slow or completely blocked drain is more than a nuisance. Left untreated, partial blockages accumulate over time, eventually causing complete backups, pipe damage, sewage overflow, and even structural damage from persistent moisture. Store-bought chemical drain cleaners provide only temporary relief — and the harsh chemicals can actually corrode older pipes, making the problem worse.

QuickFix Plumbing provides professional drain cleaning and clog removal services throughout Austin, TX that go beyond the surface-level fix. We identify the root cause, clear the obstruction completely, and inspect the line to ensure long-term results.

Drains We Service

Our drain cleaning technicians are equipped to service every drain in your home or commercial property:

  • Kitchen sink drains — Grease, food particles, and soap scum are the leading culprits in kitchen drain clogs. We cut through the buildup completely.
  • Bathroom sink, tub, and shower drains — Hair and soap residue create stubborn clogs that snaking and hydro-jetting clear quickly.

  • Commercial drain systems — Restaurants, retail spaces, and office buildings have higher volume demands; our commercial-grade equipment handles them effectively.

Our Drain Cleaning Methods

Drain Snaking (Mechanical Augering)

For straightforward clogs close to the drain opening, our motorized drain snakes break up and retrieve blockages quickly. This is often the right solution for hair clogs in bathroom drains or minor food debris in kitchen sinks.

Hydro-Jetting

For more stubborn or recurring clogs — and for main sewer line cleaning — hydro-jetting is the gold standard. Using water pressurized up to 4,000 PSI, our hydro-jetting equipment blasts through grease, scale, mineral buildup, tree root intrusion, and virtually any other obstruction. Unlike snaking, hydro-jetting doesn't just punch a hole through the clog; it scrubs the entire interior pipe wall clean.

Hydro-jetting is especially effective for:

  • Grease-lined kitchen drain pipes
  • Tree root intrusion in sewer lines
  • Mineral scale buildup from Austin's hard water
  • Recurring clogs that come back after standard snaking

Video Camera Pipe Inspection

For persistent or unexplained drainage problems, we insert a high-definition waterproof camera into your drain lines. This allows our technicians to see exactly what's causing the problem — whether it's a grease buildup, tree root invasion, collapsed pipe section, or misaligned joint — before recommending a solution. You'll see exactly what we see on a monitor, and we can provide a recording for your records.

Why Clogs Keep Coming Back

If you find yourself calling a plumber for the same drain repeatedly, the problem likely isn't a simple clog — it's a systemic issue. Common causes of recurring drain problems in Austin homes include:

  • Grease accumulation in kitchen lines that was never fully removed
  • Tree root intrusion — Austin's live oak and cedar elm trees have aggressive root systems that invade sewer lines through tiny cracks or joints
  • Pipe scale and mineral deposits from Austin's moderately hard water narrowing the pipe interior
  • Sagging or misaligned pipe sections that collect debris and slow flow regardless of cleaning
  • Venting problems that create negative pressure and slow drainage throughout the system
Our camera inspection service identifies the true culprit so we can provide a permanent solution rather than a temporary fix.

Preventive Drain Maintenance for Austin Homes

The most cost-effective drain strategy is prevention. QuickFix Plumbing offers annual drain cleaning maintenance plans for Austin homeowners and businesses. Regular professional cleaning removes buildup before it becomes a blockage, dramatically reducing the likelihood of emergency calls and extending the life of your pipes.

Simple steps to reduce drain clogs between professional cleanings:

  • Use a hair catcher in shower and tub drains
  • Never pour grease or cooking oil down the kitchen sink
  • Run hot water after each use to help flush the drain
  • Avoid flushing anything other than toilet paper down toilets

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if I have a main sewer line clog versus a single drain clog?
The clearest indicator of a main sewer line blockage is when multiple drains in your home are slow or backing up simultaneously. You might also notice gurgling sounds coming from your toilet when you run the bathroom sink, or water backing up into your bathtub when you flush the toilet. A single slow drain typically points to a localized clog in that specific line. If you're experiencing multiple affected drains, call us immediately — a main line backup can quickly become a sewage overflow inside your home.
Are chemical drain cleaners safe to use before calling a plumber?
We strongly advise against using chemical drain cleaners like Drano or Liquid-Plumr as more than a very temporary measure. These products contain highly caustic chemicals (sulfuric acid or lye) that can soften and degrade PVC pipes, corrode metal pipes, and damage pipe joints and seals over time. More importantly, they rarely dissolve the core of a clog — they just eat a temporary channel through it. If the clog persists and our technician needs to work on the drain, standing caustic chemical water in the pipe creates a serious safety hazard. Professional cleaning is always the better long-term choice.
How long does a professional drain cleaning service take?
Most single-drain cleaning appointments take 30–60 minutes. A main sewer line cleaning with hydro-jetting typically takes 1–2 hours. If we conduct a video camera inspection as part of the service, add another 30–45 minutes. We'll give you a time estimate when you schedule and our technicians respect your time — they work efficiently and clean up completely before leaving.
Can tree roots really get into my sewer line in Austin?
Absolutely, and it's actually one of the most common sewer line problems we see in Austin. The city's mature live oaks, cedar elms, and pecan trees have extensive, aggressive root systems. Roots naturally seek out moisture and will work their way into even hairline cracks in clay, cast iron, or older PVC sewer pipes. Once inside, they grow rapidly and can completely block the pipe or cause it to collapse. Hydro-jetting removes roots already inside the pipe, and we can apply a root-inhibiting treatment after cleaning. In severe cases, pipe lining or replacement may be necessary.
How often should I have my drains professionally cleaned?
For most Austin households, an annual professional drain cleaning is an excellent preventive measure that keeps lines clear before major buildup occurs. Households with large families, older pipes, or mature trees in the yard may benefit from cleanings every 6 months. Commercial properties — especially restaurants — often require quarterly or even monthly drain maintenance due to high volumes of grease and food waste. Ask us about our maintenance plans, which offer discounted rates for scheduled recurring service.
Why does my drain smell bad even when it's not clogged?
Drain odors without an apparent clog typically have one of several causes. The most common is biofilm — a layer of bacteria, soap scum, and organic matter coating the interior of the drain pipe. A professional cleaning removes this buildup and eliminates the odor at its source. Other causes include a dry or damaged P-trap (the U-shaped pipe below your drain that blocks sewer gases — if it dries out from infrequent use, sewer gas enters through the drain), a blocked or improperly installed vent pipe, or in more serious cases, a crack in the sewer line allowing gases to escape. We can diagnose the exact cause and recommend the appropriate fix.
